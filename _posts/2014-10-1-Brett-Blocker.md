a home on the Internet. This post is in response to my own piece on the

### What are you working on? 

I have an article in the works under the working title of: “On Ditching the Mental Hospital (to get drunk),”
though progress has been difficult to measure as I’ve rewritten it so
many times now. I wanted to wait until I’ve distanced myself enough from
the experiences to  write about it without relapsing into
self-destructive behavior.

I’m drawn to the first-person narrative, and for the sake of my own
ability to cope with the recent slough of manic-depressive episodes,
hospitalizations, delusions, and compulsive self-mutilation, I need to
write the aforementioned piece to make sense of my own life.

The main struggle I’m having with this piece is with the tone.  My
writing tends to be comedic and light-hearted, but I’m having difficulty
branching out into a genre that is the polar opposite of what I’m
accustomed to.

Ultimately, I would like to write something beautiful and pure, but I
might not have the wisdom for that at this stage in my life.

### How Does Your Work Differ From Others of Its Genre? 

Well actually, I’m not entirely sure what genre I fit into, but my writing is recognizable
for its blunt and generally unflattering portrayal of noteworthy
episodes in my life. I’ll also be the first to admit that I don’t even
bother with taking an objective stance on any subject.  I keep my
writing as raw as I possibly can, and if this means revealing my own
personal and dark insecurities, so be it.

I’ve come to realize people are all so fucked up in their own way that
unless you’re touching kids at a playground, they are not in a position
to judge. I rely on this principle to get away with writing the kind of
smut I do.

### -> Why Do You Write What You Do? <-

I actually hate to write, it just feels
good after the fact. Really, really good.

I would much rather be a musician or a painter.  I envy those mediums of
expression very much. But everyone’s good at something and for better or
worse, I got stuck with the written word as my medium.

I write the narrative because of my extreme jealousy of those who can so
accurately portray an experience or emotion through a creative outlet. 
The narrative is the closest I will come to letting someone take a ride
in my mind for a while, letting them in on my voice, emotions, and life
as I perceive it.

I want to be heard and acknowledged.

### How Does Your Writing Process Work? 

I’m sure this is a sign of
immaturity in my “craft,” but I don’t have a process.  Sometimes I just
sit and write all day until I’m burnt-out for weeks.  Other times, my
muse comes right away and I whip out something I’m proud of in an hour. 
Basically, I write when I feel like it.

However, what inhibits my ability to write is stress and unfinished
tasks that dangle over my head.  Right now I have an unfinished theory
paper that’s due for an incomplete class from last semester that has
practically given me an ulcer.  I’m also supposed to be going to
out-patient D.B.T therapy but haven’t out of dread and laziness.

As of now, my life is the most scattered and confusing it’s ever been,
so I enjoy writing as a means of escapism, but find it difficult to
focus with all of the mental health issues I’ve been dealing with.  I
also get the suspicion that all of the lithium, klonopin and prozac I
have to take have been killing my creativity.  But I don’t know for
sure.
